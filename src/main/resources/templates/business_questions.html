Welcome to Establishment Incident Reporting

Guidelines for Using the Bloomington Police Department Online Reporting System.

Note: Filing a false police report is a crime and is punishable by up to 180 days in jail and a fine of up to $1000.

The following are the incident types that can be reported through this system:

  • Theft
  • Vandalism

Please answer the following questions carefully to create your report.

1. I am an employee/representative of the establishment where the incident occurred?

  • Yes
  • No

2. I understand that I am reporting this incident to the Bloomington Police Department (BPD) and do not require additional assistance from the BPD at this time?

  • Yes
  • No

3. Do you require a law enforcement officer or representative from BPD to report to your location regarding this incident?

  • Yes
  • No

4. I will make contact with the Monroe County Prosecutor's Office through my establishment's process, regarding this incident and prosecution of the offender(s) following the completion of this report?

  • Yes
  • No

To add an incident you would go through a number of steps. The following information is needed to complete an incident.

  • Incident address
  • Establishment address
  • Establishment and representative contact information
  • Incident details: type, date, time, etc
  • Information on the offender(s) involved in the incident, if known
  • Information on the stolen/vandalized property(ies) (if applicable)
  • Photo(s)/receipt(s) of damaged property(ies)

After you complete and submit the online incident report, you will receive a notification email to the email address you provide stating your submission has been received. BPD staff will then process your submission. You will receive an email notification from BPD staff with a case number if your submission is approved or an email explaining why your submission was rejected..

Please make sure you have all the needed information before you continue
